What Are Dental Implants?

Dental implants are titanium posts that are surgically placed into your jawbone, where they act as artificial tooth roots. Once the implant has fused with your bone, we attach a custom crown, creating a complete tooth replacement that is incredibly strong and natural-looking.

  • Biocompatible titanium material
  • Integrates with natural bone structure
  • Provides stable foundation for replacement teeth
  • Can support single crowns, bridges, or dentures
  • Success rate of over 95%

Single Tooth Implants

If you're missing a single tooth, a dental implant with a custom crown is the ideal solution. Unlike a traditional bridge, an implant doesn't require altering adjacent healthy teeth.

  • Replace one missing tooth permanently
  • Preserve adjacent natural teeth
  • Prevent bone loss in the jaw
  • Natural appearance and function
  • No special maintenance required

Multiple Teeth Implants

For patients missing several teeth, implant-supported bridges offer a stable, comfortable solution without the drawbacks of removable partial dentures.

  • Replace multiple missing teeth
  • More stable than traditional bridges
  • No need for clasps or adhesives
  • Improved chewing ability
  • Long-lasting restoration

Implant-Supported Dentures

For patients missing all teeth in an arch, implant-supported dentures provide exceptional stability and comfort compared to traditional dentures. Say goodbye to slipping, clicking, and messy adhesives.

  • Secure fit that doesn't slip
  • Improved chewing efficiency
  • Preserve facial structure and appearance
  • Increased confidence when eating and speaking
  • Can be fixed (permanent) or removable
  • Prevent bone deterioration

The Implant Process

Getting dental implants is a multi-step process that typically takes several months, but the results are worth the wait. We'll guide you through each phase with care and expertise.

  • Comprehensive consultation and examination
  • 3D imaging and treatment planning
  • Surgical placement of implant post
  • Healing period (3-6 months) for osseointegration
  • Placement of abutment connector
  • Custom crown or restoration fabrication and placement
  • Follow-up care and maintenance guidance

Bone Grafting

If you've been missing teeth for some time, you may have experienced bone loss in your jaw. Bone grafting can restore the necessary bone volume to support dental implants successfully.

  • Rebuild jawbone density and volume
  • Create stable foundation for implants
  • Various grafting materials available
  • Can often be done at time of extraction
  • Increases implant success rate

Caring for Your Implants

One of the great advantages of dental implants is that they require the same care as natural teeth. With good oral hygiene and regular dental visits, your implants can last a lifetime.

  • Brush and floss regularly
  • Attend regular dental checkups and cleanings
  • Avoid smoking for optimal implant health
  • Use a soft-bristled toothbrush
  • Consider a water flosser for thorough cleaning
  • Protect implants from grinding with a night guard if needed

Are You a Candidate?

Most people with missing teeth are good candidates for dental implants. The ideal candidate has good overall health, sufficient bone density, and healthy gums. We'll evaluate your specific situation during your consultation.

  • Good general and oral health
  • Adequate bone density (or candidate for bone grafting)
  • Healthy gum tissue
  • Commitment to good oral hygiene
  • Non-smoker or willing to quit
  • Realistic expectations about the process and results
